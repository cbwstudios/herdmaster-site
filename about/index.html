Our story

Built by a breeder, for breeders.

HerdMaster was created by Joe Kim, an ARBA Convention Best in Show winner and a life member of the American Rabbit Breeders Association, after 45 years in the barn. Here is how it started, in his words.

Where it started

A kid who just wanted
to fit in.

I came to the United States at five years old, speaking only Korean and Japanese, and grew up in Terre Haute, Indiana, one of the few Asian families in town. For years I was 'that foreign kid.' I begged my parents for a dog and got a fish tank instead, so I bred show guppies and bettas and learned to cull by what I read. Then I found Bob Bennett's 'Raising Rabbits the Modern Way,' and that book led me to the Vigo County 4-H Rabbit Club. For the first time I wasn't the outsider. I was just a kid who loved rabbits, surrounded by other kids who loved rabbits. Nobody cared where I was born.

"I got my life started with 4-H."

On the show table

Forty-five years in, and still nervous at every show.

I got serious again in 2000 in Malibu, California, which is where the Malibu Rabbitry prefix comes from. I won Best in Show at the 2003 ARBA Convention with Tans and put together a streak of six straight Best of Breed wins at Convention. Years later I came back, won Best of Breed at the 2018 Convention, then moved to Mini Rex and took Best of Breed at the 2021 Convention out of more than a thousand entries. But the win I am proudest of is not mine: four of the last five national and convention winners were made on Malibu lines. Helping other breeders succeed has always meant more to me than another ribbon.

ARBA Convention Best in Show 2x Convention Best of Group 11x Convention Best of Breed National show winners since 2001
Why HerdMaster

The tool I wish I'd had.

In 2023 I hit my head and started bleeding in the brain. I came out weaker and slower, but alive. I spent that stretch in the Neuro ICU on my phone, logging future breedings on a spreadsheet, because I did not want to face reality. I have also detached my retina twice. I cannot trim nails or tattoo the way I used to, and my vision slips a little every year. I know the day is coming when I cannot breed at top form. That spreadsheet in the hospital became HerdMaster. I am building the tool I wish I had, so that serious record keeping takes less of the time and eyesight our rabbits deserve, and so I can give back to a community that has given me everything, while I still can.

"I was holed up in the Neuro ICU on my phone with my spreadsheet, logging future breedings."
